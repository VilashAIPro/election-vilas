    </div>
    <div class="india-stat-card">
      <div class="isc-badge">Electorate</div>
      <div class="isc-num">969M+</div>
      <div class="isc-label">Registered voters — world's largest democratic electorate</div>
    </div>
    <div class="india-stat-card">
      <div class="isc-badge">Technology</div>
      <div class="isc-num">EVM</div>
      <div class="isc-label">Used since 2004 — standalone, non-networked voting machines</div>
    </div>
    <div class="india-stat-card">
      <div class="isc-badge">Option</div>
      <div class="isc-num">NOTA</div>
      <div class="isc-label">None of the Above — available on every EVM since 2013</div>
    </div>
    <div class="india-stat-card">
      <div class="isc-badge">Body</div>
      <div class="isc-num">ECI</div>
      <div class="isc-label">Constitutional body under Article 324 — fully independent</div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <h3 style="font-family:'Playfair Display',serif;font-size:22px;margin-bottom:20px">India-specific rules</h3>
  <div class="india-rules">
    <div class="india-rule">
      <div class="india-rule-term">Model Code of Conduct (MCC)</div>
      <div class="india-rule-def">Activated immediately when election dates are announced. Restricts the government from announcing new welfare schemes, making official transfers, or using state funds/machinery for campaigning. Violating the MCC can lead to show-cause notices from ECI.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="india-rule">
      <div class="india-rule-term">Alternate voter ID (12 documents accepted)</div>
      <div class="india-rule-def">If you don't have an EPIC card, you may use: Aadhaar, Passport, Driving Licence, MNREGA Job Card, Bank/Post Office Passbook with photo, Smart Card issued by Ministry of Labour, CGHS/ECHS photo card, Pension document with photo, or official identity document issued by Government of India/State.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="india-rule">
      <div class="india-rule-term">Campaign expenditure limits</div>
      <div class="india-rule-def">Lok Sabha: ₹95 lakh per candidate (larger states), ₹75 lakh (smaller states/UTs). State Assembly: ₹40 lakh per candidate (larger states). Expenses are tracked by ECI-appointed expenditure observers. Violation can lead to disqualification under RPA 1951.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="india-rule">
      <div class="india-rule-term">Anti-defection law (10th Schedule)</div>
      <div class="india-rule-def">MPs and MLAs who switch parties or vote against their party whip without sanction can be disqualified. The Speaker (Lok Sabha) or Chairman (Rajya Sabha) adjudicates defection cases. This was introduced by the 52nd Constitutional Amendment in 1985.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="india-rule">
      <div class="india-rule-term">VVPAT verification</div>
      <div class="india-rule-def">Since 2019, every EVM is paired with a Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail machine. After you press the EVM button, a paper slip appears behind a glass screen for 7 seconds showing your candidate's name and symbol before dropping into a sealed box. This adds a physical audit trail to electronic voting.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="india-rule">
      <div class="india-rule-term">Paid news prohibition</div>
      <div class="india-rule-def">Publishing or broadcasting any news or article for payment without disclosure as an advertisement is prohibited and treated as a corrupt practice under the Representation of the People Act. ECI monitors media during elections through Media Certification and Monitoring Committees (MCMCs).</div>
    </div>
  </div>

<section id="types">
  <div class="section-tag">05 — Types</div>
  <h2 class="section-title">Types of elections in India</h2>
  <p class="section-sub">India holds several distinct types of elections across national, state, and local levels.</p>
  <div class="types-grid">
    <div class="type-card">
      <div class="type-tag tag-blue">National</div>
      <h3>General Election (Lok Sabha)</h3>
      <p>Elects 543 MPs every 5 years. Determines which party or coalition forms the Central Government and who becomes Prime Minister.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="type-card">
      <div class="type-tag tag-teal">State</div>
      <h3>State Assembly (Vidhan Sabha)</h3>
      <p>Each state elects its legislative assembly. The majority party forms the state government led by the Chief Minister. Timing varies by state.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="type-card">
      <div class="type-tag tag-purple">Upper House</div>
      <h3>Rajya Sabha Election</h3>
      <p>Members are indirectly elected by state legislative assemblies using single transferable vote. One-third retire every two years. Citizens do not vote directly.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="type-card">
      <div class="type-tag tag-green">Local Body</div>
      <h3>Panchayat / Municipal</h3>
      <p>Elections for gram panchayats, panchayat samitis, town municipalities, and city corporations. Conducted by State Election Commissions, not ECI.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="type-card">
      <div class="type-tag tag-amber">Interim</div>
      <h3>By-Election (Upchunav)</h3>
      <p>Held to fill a vacant MP or MLA seat caused by death, disqualification, resignation, or court order. Smaller in scope than a general election.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="type-card">
      <div class="type-tag tag-coral">Executive</div>
      <h3>Presidential Election</h3>
      <p>India's President is chosen by an Electoral College of elected MPs and MLAs using the single transferable vote system — not by direct public vote.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section id="glossary">
  <div class="section-tag">06 — Glossary</div>
  <h2 class="section-title">Key election terms</h2>
  <p class="section-sub">Essential vocabulary for understanding the Indian election process.</p>
  <div class="glos-grid">
    <div class="glos-item"><div class="glos-term">EVM (Electronic Voting Machine)</div><div class="glos-def">A standalone, non-networked electronic device used to record votes in India since 2004. Contains a one-time programmable chip. Cannot be hacked remotely. Paired with VVPAT for transparency since 2019.</div></div>
    <div class="glos-item"><div class="glos-term">VVPAT</div><div class="glos-def">Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail — a machine attached to the EVM that prints a paper slip showing the voter's chosen candidate for 7 seconds before it drops into a sealed compartment. Provides a physical audit trail.</div></div>
    <div class="glos-item"><div class="glos-term">EPIC (Voter ID Card)</div><div class="glos-def">Elector's Photo Identity Card — the official voter identification document issued by ECI. Required at polling booths, though 12 alternate IDs are also accepted.</div></div>
    <div class="glos-item"><div class="glos-term">Electoral Roll</div><div class="glos-def">The official list of all registered voters in a constituency. Published and updated by Electoral Registration Officers. You must appear on this list to vote. Verifiable at electoralsearch.in.</div></div>
    <div class="glos-item"><div class="glos-term">First-Past-The-Post (FPTP)</div><div class="glos-def">India's voting system — the candidate with the most votes in a constituency wins, even without an absolute majority. A candidate can win with as little as 25–30% of votes if others split the rest.</div></div>
    <div class="glos-item"><div class="glos-term">Model Code of Conduct</div><div class="glos-def">A set of guidelines issued by ECI that governs political parties and candidates during elections. Comes into force on the day elections are announced and stays until results are declared.</div></div>
    <div class="glos-item"><div class="glos-term">NOTA (None of the Above)</div><div class="glos-def">An option on every Indian EVM since 2013 allowing voters to reject all candidates on the ballot. NOTA votes are counted and announced but do not affect the result — the candidate with the highest non-NOTA votes wins.</div></div>
    <div class="glos-item"><div class="glos-term">Returning Officer</div><div class="glos-def">The District Magistrate or senior official designated by ECI to manage the election in one constituency — overseeing nominations, polling, and officially declaring the result.</div></div>
    <div class="glos-item"><div class="glos-term">Hung Parliament</div><div class="glos-def">When no single party wins 272+ seats (simple majority in the 543-seat Lok Sabha). Coalition governments are formed through negotiation. India has seen hung parliaments in 1989, 1996, 1998, 1999, and 2009.</div></div>
    <div class="glos-item"><div class="glos-term">Exit Poll</div><div class="glos-def">A survey of voters conducted right after they vote. In multi-phase Indian elections, exit polls cannot be published or broadcast until the final phase of polling ends — to avoid influencing later-phase voters.</div></div>
  </div>
</section>

<section id="faq">
  <div class="section-tag">07 — FAQ</div>
  <h2 class="section-title">Frequently asked questions</h2>
  <p class="section-sub">Answers to the most common questions about voting in India.</p>
  <div class="faq-list">
    <div class="faq-item">
      <div class="faq-q" onclick="toggleFAQ(this)">Who is eligible to vote in India? <span class="faq-icon">+</span></div>
      <div class="faq-a"><div class="faq-text">Any Indian citizen aged 18 or above who is registered in the electoral roll of their constituency. NRIs holding Indian passports can register and must vote in person at their home constituency (postal/proxy voting not available for NRIs). People declared of unsound mind by a court, and those imprisoned and serving a sentence are excluded.</div></div>
    </div>
    <div class="faq-item">
      <div class="faq-q" onclick="toggleFAQ(this)">Can I vote without a Voter ID card? <span class="faq-icon">+</span></div>
      <div class="faq-a"><div class="faq-text">Yes. ECI accepts 12 alternate photo ID documents: Aadhaar card, Passport, Driving Licence, MNREGA job card, Bank/Post Office passbook with photo, Smart Card (ECHS/CGHS), Pension document with photo, and others. However, your name must still appear on the electoral roll — the ID only verifies your identity at the booth.</div></div>
    </div>
    <div class="faq-item">
      <div class="faq-q" onclick="toggleFAQ(this)">Are EVMs tamper-proof and reliable? <span class="faq-icon">+</span></div>
      <div class="faq-a"><div class="faq-text">EVMs use one-time programmable microcontrollers and are not connected to the internet or any network. They are sealed before and after polling and stored under armed guard. The VVPAT adds a paper verification layer. The Supreme Court has upheld EVM validity in multiple cases, including in 2024, declining to revert to paper ballots.</div></div>

let countdowns=[];
let activeIdx=-1;
let cdInterval=null;
function addPreset(name,date){document.getElementById('cdElectionName').value=name;document.getElementById('cdDate').value=date;addCountdown();}
function addCountdown(){
  const name=document.getElementById('cdElectionName').value.trim();
  const date=document.getElementById('cdDate').value;
  if(!name||!date)return;
  countdowns.push({name,date});
  document.getElementById('cdElectionName').value='';
  document.getElementById('cdDate').value='';
  renderSaved();
  setActive(countdowns.length-1);
}
function renderSaved(){
  const list=document.getElementById('savedList');
  list.innerHTML=countdowns.map((c,i)=>`<div class="saved-item${activeIdx===i?' active-item':''}" onclick="setActive(${i})"><span class="saved-name">${c.name}</span><span class="saved-date">${new Date(c.date).toLocaleDateString('en-IN',{day:'numeric',month:'short',year:'numeric'})}</span><button class="saved-del" onclick="event.stopPropagation();deleteCd(${i})">Remove</button></div>`).join('');
}
function deleteCd(i){countdowns.splice(i,1);if(activeIdx>=countdowns.length)activeIdx=countdowns.length-1;renderSaved();if(activeIdx>=0)setActive(activeIdx);else{clearInterval(cdInterval);document.getElementById('cdDisplay').classList.remove('visible');}}
function setActive(i){
  activeIdx=i;
  renderSaved();
  clearInterval(cdInterval);
  const target=new Date(countdowns[i].date+'T00:00:00');
  document.getElementById('cdName').textContent=countdowns[i].name;
  document.getElementById('cdDisplay').classList.add('visible');
  function tick(){
    const now=new Date();
    const diff=target-now;
    if(diff<=0){document.getElementById('cdGrid').innerHTML='<div class="cd-expired">This election date has passed.</div>';return;}
    const d=Math.floor(diff/86400000);
    const h=Math.floor((diff%86400000)/3600000);
    const m=Math.floor((diff%3600000)/60000);
    const s=Math.floor((diff%60000)/1000);
    document.getElementById('cdDays').textContent=String(d).padStart(2,'0');
    document.getElementById('cdHours').textContent=String(h).padStart(2,'0');
    document.getElementById('cdMins').textContent=String(m).padStart(2,'0');
    document.getElementById('cdSecs').textContent=String(s).padStart(2,'0');
    document.getElementById('cdDateLbl').textContent='Target: '+target.toLocaleDateString('en-IN',{weekday:'long',day:'numeric',month:'long',year:'numeric'});
    document.getElementById('cdGrid').innerHTML=`<div class="cd-unit"><div class="cd-num">${String(d).padStart(2,'0')}</div><div class="cd-lbl">Days</div></div><div class="cd-unit"><div class="cd-num">${String(h).padStart(2,'0')}</div><div class="cd-lbl">Hours</div></div><div class="cd-unit"><div class="cd-num">${String(m).padStart(2,'0')}</div><div class="cd-lbl">Minutes</div></div><div class="cd-unit"><div class="cd-num">${String(s).padStart(2,'0')}</div><div class="cd-lbl">Seconds</div></div>`;
  }
  tick();cdInterval=setInterval(tick,1000);
}
